# Make the point clear and worth reading

Review the exact final candidate after the human-language edit.

1. State who the copy is for and what they should understand, feel, or do next.
2. Put the strongest true benefit near the front.
3. Prefer concrete outcomes and mechanisms over category labels and adjectives.
4. Keep one main promise per title, headline, or short block.
5. Add proof only when it is real and available. Remove any claim the evidence cannot support.
6. Cut jargon, hype, and words that do not help a distracted reader decide.
7. Check that the result is accurate, specific, and easy to understand on the first read.

Return the approved copy and a short safe evidence ID beginning with `ogilvy:`. The ID may name the check, such as `ogilvy:benefit-proof`, but it must not contain the copy, prompt, a person's name, or private context.
