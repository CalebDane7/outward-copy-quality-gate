# Make the copy sound written by a person

Edit the exact candidate copy, not a summary of it.

1. Preserve the factual meaning, audience, and requested action.
2. Replace inflated or abstract language with specific nouns and direct verbs.
3. Remove filler, canned openings and closings, fake enthusiasm, vague authorities, and claims without evidence.
4. Break mechanical repetition. Keep sentence length and structure natural for the surface instead of forcing a pattern.
5. Remove formatting that exists only to look polished. Keep headings and lists only when they help the reader scan.
6. Read the result aloud once. Fix any line a person would stumble over or skip.

Return the revised copy. Do not add a receipt, evidence ID, or hidden marker to the user's text.
